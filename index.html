Research

I am currently focused on research in AI for Science.

My areas of interest span physical scales, from weather and climate science to materials discovery and molecular biology. Recent publications:

  • Accelerating the Generation of Molecular Conformations with Progressive Distillation of Equivariant Latent Diffusion Models, ICLR 2024 Generative and Experimental Perspectives for Biomolecular Design Workshop; and Stanford Graph Learning Workshop, 2024. [arxiv][github]
  • AdsorbRL: Deep Reinforcement Learning for Inverse Catalyst Design, NeurIPS 2023 AI for Materials Design Workshop. [arxiv][github]
  • ClimateX: Do LLMs Accurately Assess Human Expert Confidence in Climate Statements?, Tackling Climate Change with Machine Learning Workshop, NeurIPS 2023. [arxiv][github]
  • Extracting Molecular Properties from Natural Language with Multimodal Contrastive Learning, ICML 2023 Computational Biology Workshop; and AI for Organic Chemistry Workshop, ACS Fall 2023. [arxiv][github]
  • Improving Extreme Weather Events Detection with Light-Weight Neural Networks, ICLR 2023 Tackling Climate Change with Machine Learning Workshop. [arxiv][github]
I've served as reviewer and on the program committee for NeurIPS and ICLR workshops on Climate Change, Life and Material Science, and Foundation Models for Biological Discovery.

Previous work

  • I'm a TED Fellow and introduced our work on air quality in this talk at TED 2018.
  • From 2011 to 2014 I launched data.gouv.fr to open government data in my native France.
  • I studied math and physics at Ecole Polytechnique, engineering systems at MIT, and chemical engineering at Stanford.

Technology investing
